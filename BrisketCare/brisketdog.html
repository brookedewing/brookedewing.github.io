Food and Treats

  • Regular Food: Slightly over 1.5 cups, twice per day
  • - Timing isn't super important, he usually gets breakfast whenever we get up (anywhere between 7:30am and 9:30am) and has dinner around when we eat (anywhere between 5:30pm and 7:00pm)
  • - FYI As he gets older, he doesn't always eat his meals right away. This is fine! If he isn't finishing his food by end of day, just let us know though :)
  • Treats:
  • - Our training treats are super low-cal, so feel free to give him these as needed for coercion or when you feel like having him do some tricks.
  • - Dental Treats are also a daily snack for him! I still need to pick some up, but I generally get a type called "Fresh Kisses" and he will get really excited if you ask him if he wants one. I usually give to him mid-morning or whenever I think about it!
  • - He also gets one some kind of treat when getting in bed every night :) We've mainly just been doing a couple of training treats lately.

Routines

  • Exercise: You don't need to stick to anything here, but just so you know what he's used to: we generally do either two smaller outings (e.g. mile or so walk) or one larger one (longer walk or play time at dog park) each day
  • - For example, if I'm just doing one long walk with him, I'll take him from our house up to Roanoke Park and play ball/chase him around, then we'll do the trails that go around the park! (probably about 3ish miles total).
  • - If I remember, I'll incude his weeble wobble that you can fill with breakfast or dinner and add a few treats. This can entertain him for awhile- it's not exactly exercise, but can help if he's bored!
  • Bathroom Breaks: He usually goes out right after we get up and right before we go to bed, but other than that he doesn't really have a potty routine. I don't pay much attention to know how many times he goes per day, but he generally seems to poop about twice a day :)
  • - Just a warning - if it's raining, he won't want to go out. Don't worry too much though, he can hold it for a REALLY long time.
  • Kennel: We stopped using his kennel about six months or so ago and he hasn't had any issues!

Tricks and Accompanying Hand Signals

  • Sit: Palm facing self and fist upwards (e.g. closed fist facing yourself if that makes more sense)
  • Stay: Hand in Stop Sign position
  • Shake: Hand in Low-Five Position
  • Shake (Other Paw): After asking him to shake one paw, switch your hands and say "Other Paw"
  • High Five: Hand in High-Five Position ("Other Paw" also works for this)
  • Lay Down: Hold food toward floor
  • Spin: Clockwise circular motion over his head.

Key Words

  • Get Ready: You can say "should I get ready?" if you want to get him hyped for a walk (he also knows the word walk!)
  • Bedtime: He knows this means one more chance to go to the bathroom and then a treat and sleep time is coming.
  • Go Potty: Pretty self-explanatory. Use this if he gets distracted while you're trying to get him to go to the bathroom. We also feel a bit silly saying this one if that makes you feel any better...
  • Can you come here?: Sometimes more effective than just "come here" for some reason.
  • Let's Go: Phrase for getting him to move along on a walk if he stops to sniff or something for too long
  • Drop it/Can I have it?: If he has something in his mouth you want or that shouldn't be in there
  • Leave it: If he's sniffing something gross or won't let you pick up a ball while playing/etc.
  • Can you get in your kennel?: Self-explanatory!
  • Can you get your... : e.g. ball, toy, etc.
